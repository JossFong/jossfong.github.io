Racial disparity of incarceration varies by state

In Wisconsin and New Jersey, the incarceration rate of black men is more than 15 times the incarceration rate of white men

Governments in the United States lock up a larger chunk of the national population than any other country in the world. Minorities, and black men in particular, account for a disproportionate share of the prison population. This map shows the black/white disparity for incarceration rates in each state, drawing on data compiled by the Kaiser Family Foundation.
